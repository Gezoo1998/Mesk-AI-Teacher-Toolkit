Here are **20 high-impact AI tools** we can build to expand the toolkit, categorized by teacher needs.

## üìù Assessment & Grading
1.  **Smart Rubric Generator**
    *   **Input**: Grade level, Subject, Assignment description (e.g., "Persuasive Essay").
    *   **Output**: A detailed table with criteria (Grammar, Argument, Evidence) and scoring levels (4, 3, 2, 1).
2.  **Quiz Wizard**
    *   **Input**: Topic or Paste Text (e.g., "The Water Cycle").
    *   **Output**: 10 Multiple Choice Questions, True/False, and Short Answer questions with Answer Keys.
3.  **Exit Ticket Designer**
    *   **Input**: Lesson objective.
    *   **Output**: 3 quick questions to check understanding at the end of class.

## ü§ù Communication & Admin
4.  **Parent Email Drafter**
    *   **Input**: Student name, Issue (e.g., "Missing homework", "Did great today"), Tone (Professional, Warm).
    *   **Output**: A perfectly polished email ready to send.
5.  **Report Card Comment Bank**
    *   **Input**: Student strengths/weaknesses.
    *   **Output**: Personalized, constructive paragraphs for report cards.
6.  **Permission Slip Generator**
    *   **Input**: Event details, Date, Cost.
    *   **Output**: A complete permission form text.

## üß† Differentiation & Accessibility
7.  **Reading Level Adjuster (The "Leveler")**
    *   **Input**: Complex text.
    *   **Output**: The same text rewritten for Lower, Middle, and Higher reading levels simultaneously.
8.  **IEP Goal Assistant**
    *   **Input**: Student need (e.g., "Reading fluency").
    *   **Output**: SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
9.  **ESL/ELL Scaffolder**
    *   **Input**: Vocabulary words or Lesson topic.
    *   **Output**: Sentence frames, visual descriptions, and cognates (English/Arabic) to support learners.

## üé® Creative & Engaging
10. **"Hook" Generator**
    *   **Input**: Boring topic (e.g., "Punctuation").
    *   **Output**: 5 exciting ways to start the lesson (a mystery, a joke, a demonstration).
11. **Project-Based Learning (PBL) Planner**
    *   **Input**: Real-world problem key topic.
    *   **Output**: A 2-week project outline with milestones, driving question, and final product ideas.
12. **Classroom Icebreakers**
    *   **Input**: Group size, Time available.
    *   **Output**: Fun, low-stakes activities to build community.
13. **Debate Topic Spark**
    *   **Input**: Subject area.
    *   **Output**: 5 controversial (but age-appropriate) statements to get students arguing constructively.

    *   **Input**: List of 10 vocabulary words.
    *   **Output**: A coherent, funny short story using all 10 words in context.
