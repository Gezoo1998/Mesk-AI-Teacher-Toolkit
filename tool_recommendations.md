## üßÆ For Math Teachers

### 1. "Step-by-Step Solver" (Explanation Generator)
**Problem**: Teachers often need to show *how* to solve a problem in multiple ways, not just the answer.
**Function**:
-   **Input**: A math problem (text).
-   **Output**: 3 different methods to solve it (e.g., standard algorithm, mental math strategy, visual model approach) with clear, simple language explaining *why* each step works.
**Why**: Helps differentiate instruction for students who learn differently.

### 2. "Real-World Math Connector"
**Problem**: Students always ask, "When will I ever use this?"
**Function**:
-   **Input**: Math Concept (e.g., Quadratic Equations, Pythagoras).
-   **Output**: 3-4 engaging real-life scenarios (Architecture, Video Game Physics, Sports Stats) where this specific math is used, complete with a mini-story hook.
**Why**: instant engagement booster.

## üî¨ For Science Teachers

### 3. "Misconception Buster"
**Problem**: Students often come with deeply ingrained scientific misconceptions (e.g., "seasons are caused by distance from the sun").
**Function**:
-   **Input**: Topic (e.g., Gravity, Evolution, Seasons).
-   **Output**: A "True vs. False" table, explaining the common myth, the scientific reality, and a simple experiment or analogy to prove the truth.
**Why**: Directly addresses learning gaps rooted in false intuition.

### 4. "Lab Safety Story Generator"
**Problem**: Safety rules are boring to read.
**Function**:
-   **Input**: Experiment Type (e.g., Acid/Base titration).
-   **Output**: A "What Went Wrong?" fictional short story where a character follows/ignores safety rules, forcing students to identify the mistakes and consequences in a safe, narrative way.
**Why**: Memorizing rules is dull; analyzing a story is active learning.
